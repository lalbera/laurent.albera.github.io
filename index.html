Research Activities

His research interests include High-Order statistics, Multidimensional Algebra, Blind deconvolution and Equalization, Digital communications, Statistical Signal and Array Processing, and Numerical analysis. More exactly, since 2000 Laurent Albera has currently worked in Blind Source Separation (BSS) and in Independent Component Analysis (ICA) by processing both the cyclostationary source case and the underdetermined mixture identification problem. He has about 14 publications to his name, and 4 patents.

 

Teaching activities

Laurent Albera has shared statistical teaching activities in French Universities of Science both in Mathematics and in Signal Processing (Orsay, Marne-La-Vallée, Rennes).
